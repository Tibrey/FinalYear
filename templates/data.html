Overview of Breast Cancer

Cancer is one of the leading causes of death globally. Breast Cancer♋ is a disease in which abnormal breast cells grow out of control and form tumors.If left unchecked, the tumors can spread through out the body and become fatal. Breast cancer originates in the milk ducts or lobules of the breast. The initial, non-life-threatening form is confined to these structures (in situ). However, cancer cells can progress to invade nearby breast tissue, forming tumors that manifest as lumps or thickening. Invasive cancers may further spread to nearby lymph nodes or other organs, posing a risk of metastasis, which can be fatal. Treatment approaches are personalized, considering factors such as the individual, cancer type, and extent of spread

Risk Factors for Breast Cancer

The primary risk factor for breast cancer is female gender, with only 0.5–1% of cases occurring in men. Treatment for male breast cancer follows the same principles as for women. Various factors, including increasing age, obesity, alcohol consumption, family history, radiation exposure, reproductive factors, tobacco use, and postmenopausal hormone therapy, elevate breast cancer risk. Approximately half of cases develop in women without identifiable risk factors beyond being female and over 40 years old. While a family history increases risk, most diagnosed women lack known familial connections to breast cancer, and the absence of such history doesn't necessarily imply reduced risk. Certain high-penetrance gene mutations, notably in BRCA1, BRCA2, and PALB-2, significantly elevate breast cancer risk. Women carrying these mutations may consider risk reduction strategies like preventive breast surgery.
